A graph of all penalty locations throughout the 2025 season (so far) tell a very interesting story. It's important to note that the "Yardline" variable means how far the offensive team is away from the opponent's end zone. Therefore, a penalty at "Yardline = 99" would have happened while the offense was backed up at their own 1-yard-line. "Yardline = 1" means the offense is at the opponent's 1-yard-line and is in prime scoring position. The NFL also doesn't track where the penalty occurs across the width of the field, so the location vertically on the field doesn't matter. The points are just spread out to make them easier to distinguish from each other.
The first thing that stands out is the line at the 35-yard-line. It may appear out-of-place at first, but looking deeper, it makes sense that so many penalties occur there. All "Kickoff Short of Landing Zone" penalties will occur at the kicking team's 35, so they are all concentrated there. It is also unsurprising to see multiple drives a game start at the 35-yard line this season. This means more plays are being run there consistently, increasing the likelihood of seeing penalties. 
There are also fewer penalties the closer you get to "Yardline = 100." This too makes sense as fewer plays are going to be ran in that area of the field. Most plays will be ran, and subsequently most penalties will occur, closer to midfield or the opponent side of the field.

In general, it appears that team penalties follow a fairly linear trend (as modeled by the red line on the graph). As the number of penalties increase, naturally so does the number of penalty yards. The data shows that on average, for every penalty, the number of yeards increases by 9.26.
No team really strays too far from the trend line, but a few stick out as above or below average. The Broncos are the furthest above the line meaning for the amount of penalties they committed, they were assessed more yards than would be expected. Teams like this generally commit more serious or costly penalties like defensive pass interference or personal foul penalties. Teams like the Saints and Cardinals were on the opposite end of the spectrum, committing less costly penalties. 
The Jaguars also seem to separate themselves in terms of number of penalties and total penalty yards - not a place any team wants to be. The Rams on the other hand, clearly have the fewest penalties and penalty yards.
